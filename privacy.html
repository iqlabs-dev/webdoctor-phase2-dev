3. Information we collect

Information you choose to provide:

  • Name and contact details (such as email address).
  • Account login details (such as username and hashed passwords).
  • Websites or URLs you choose to scan or analyse.
  • Support requests, feedback, and other communications you send to us.

Information collected automatically:

  • IP address and approximate region or city.
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system.
  • Pages viewed, actions taken in the product, and timestamps.
  • Diagnostic information related to performance, errors, and reliability.

Payment information:

We do not store full card details on iQLABS servers. All payments for our products are processed securely by Paddle.com Market Ltd (“Paddle”), our Merchant of Record. Paddle will collect billing information as needed to complete your purchase, in line with its own terms and privacy policy.

Cookies and similar technologies:

We may use cookies and similar technologies to remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and gather anonymous usage statistics. You can adjust your browser settings to block or delete cookies, although some features of our services may not function correctly if you do so.

4. How we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain our products and services.
  • Generate diagnostic scores and reports for the websites you choose to analyse.
  • Respond to enquiries, support requests, and technical issues.
  • Improve, test, and refine our products, including analytics on how features are used.
  • Protect our services and users from abuse, spam, and security threats.
  • Comply with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations.
  • Send you service-related communications (for example, security notices or important updates).

Where we send optional product news or marketing communications, you can opt out at any time using the unsubscribe link in the message or by contacting us.

5. Legal bases for processing (GDPR / UK GDPR)

Where the EU or UK data protection laws apply, we rely on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract – to provide the services you sign up for.
  • Legitimate interests – such as improving our services, preventing abuse, and operating a secure platform.
  • Consent – where required for certain optional uses (for example, some cookies or marketing).
  • Legal obligation – where we must process data to comply with applicable law.

6. How we share information

We do not sell your personal data.

We may share information with trusted third parties that help us run our services, including:

  • Paddle – to process payments, issue invoices, and manage taxes.
  • Cloud hosting, database, and storage providers.
  • Analytics, monitoring, and logging services.
  • Customer support and communication tools.
  • Providers like OpenAI where AI-powered features are used, in line with their terms.

These service providers are given access only to the information they need to perform their functions and are required to handle it appropriately.

We may also disclose information if required by law, court order, or legal process, or if we believe it is necessary to protect our rights, users, or the public.

7. International data transfers

iQLABS is based in New Zealand, and our service providers may operate in New Zealand, the European Union, the United States, or other countries. This means your information may be transferred to and processed in countries that may have different data protection laws than your own.

Where required, we take steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, such as contractual protections consistent with applicable data protection law.

8. Data retention

We keep personal information only for as long as reasonably necessary to provide our services, meet legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

  • Account data is generally kept while your account is active.
  • Diagnostic data and reports may be retained for a period to support your use of the service and our product improvements.
  • Certain records (for example, transaction details kept by Paddle) may be retained for longer where required by tax or accounting laws.

9. Security

We use technical and organisational measures to protect personal information, including encryption in transit (HTTPS), access controls, and monitoring of our infrastructure.

No online service can guarantee perfect security, but we work to protect your data and review our controls regularly. If we become aware of a significant security incident affecting your data, we will take appropriate steps and, where required by law, notify you and the relevant authorities.

Depending on your location, you may have rights over your personal information, including:

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@iqlabs.co.nz. We may need to verify your identity before responding. Some rights may be limited where we have overriding legal obligations or compelling legitimate interests.
